🚀 SpaceX IPO Market Impact Analysis | Python + Financial Analytics

I built a data analytics project to answer one question:

How different is the SpaceX IPO compared to other major IPOs, and what kind of impact can it create in the stock market?

Using Python, pandas, Matplotlib, Plotly, and Excel reporting, I analyzed SpaceX against major historical IPOs and recent tech/fintech IPOs including CoreWeave, Circle, Figma, and Chime.

Key insights:

1. SpaceX is not a normal IPO story.
It combines aerospace, reusable rockets, Starlink satellite internet, government contracts, AI infrastructure potential, retail investor attention, and global brand power.

2. IPO size changes the entire market conversation.
Even if smaller IPOs can show strong percentage returns, SpaceX creates a larger market-impact story because of scale, liquidity, investor demand, and visibility.

3. Market impact is more than stock return.
I analyzed IPO proceeds, historical IPO comparisons, offering-size multiples, investor paper-gain scenarios, Elon Musk ownership-benefit scenarios, and a custom market impact score.

4. Elon Musk benefits differently from IPO investors.
IPO investors benefit through share appreciation if they hold after allocation. Musk benefits mainly through ownership value, control, future liquidity optionality, and stronger public-market power. These are paper-value gains unless shares are sold.

Tools used:
Python | pandas | NumPy | Matplotlib | Plotly | Excel | Financial Analytics | Data Storytelling

My biggest takeaway:

A high-impact IPO is not measured only by price movement. It is measured by size, timing, investor attention, sector influence, liquidity, and the story it creates in the market.
